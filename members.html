Members
Eri Saikawa
Eri is an Associate Professor in the Department of Environmental Sciences, Emory College and jointly appointed in the Department of Environmental Health at the Rollins School of Public Health at Emory University. Her main research questions are related to the source and the magnitude of emissions linked to air pollution, ozone depletion, and global warming, as well as the impacts of these emissions on humans and on the society.
Raymond Cheng
Raymond is a third-year undergraduate student double majoring in Environmental Science and Quantitative Science with a concentration in Medical Anthropology. He’s currently enrolled in a BS/MPH program with Rollins School of Public Health in Environmental Health. He comes from Nanjing, China and joined the group in fall 2018. His research interest mainly involves environmental epidemiology, the exposure assessment and health outcome evaluation through qualitative and quantitative research methods, with a particular emphasis on that of air pollution. He’s currently involved in the cough clinic study, COVID-AQ study and also part of Air Emory group.
