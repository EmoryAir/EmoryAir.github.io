Get to Know Your Learning Atmosphere

The Saikawa lab is looking for schools to partner with to place low-cost air sensors on their campuses. We are primarily looking to partner with schools in the metro Atlanta area but are willing to work with any school in Georgia. The sensors are free of charge from Airly. The Saikawa lab will place the sensors on the school’s campus and link the sensor’s updated data reports to our website hourly.


The sensors are the basis for a larger research project the Air Emory group is currently engaged in. The group is researching the impact that air quality has on student academic performance and attendance. The project is scheduled to begin in October 2021 and end in February 2022.


So, why should your school participate?


  1. It’s Free! Quality-controlled low-cost air sensors are provided to your school free of charge through Airly. The Saikawa lab will make sure that the data is accurate and corrected against regulatory monitor data.

  2. We Do All The Work! The Saikawa lab will install the air sensors and link the reporting data to our website.The data can also be linked to the school’s website.

  3. STEM In the Classroom! This project may pique students’ interest in air quality and the impacts it can have on their health and academic performance. This project can be applied to the classroom to continue to raise awareness of air quality and further students’ interest in STEM fields.

If you are interested, please fill out this Interest Form. You will then receive an email from a member in the Saikawa lab with further information about the project and the next steps in the sensor placement process. If you have any further questions, please contact air.emory@emory.edu.
