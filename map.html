Air Emory Sensor Map

Air Emory is a student-led air quality sensor network. The first of its kind at Emory that displays real-time data around campus. This promotes awareness, general knowledge, and engagement for students and faculty alike about our interaction with the environment.

We currently have one sensor running outside at the Emory Math and Science Center, and are working on setting up more sensors around campus.



Existing Sensor Systems

The Air Emory team is currently working to install more sensors in our community. However, before we are able to build an extensive network of sensors in the area, please check out these existing sensor systems on this tab to better comprehend the air quality.

  • AirNow
  • PurpleAir
  • Airly

AirNow’s centralized data system provides quality control, national reporting consistency, and the ability to distribute data to the public, researchers, businesses, educators, and to other data systems.

AirNow has current and forecast air quality maps and data for more than 500 cities across the U.S. Check out AirNow’s new interactive map below.

AirNow Website


PurpleAir is a community air monitoring system of air pollution sensors. The PurpleAir system is composed of many sensors that are installed, controlled and maintained by members of the community.

Readings are then uploaded to the cloud every 80 seconds (or so) where they are stored for download and displayed on the PurpleAir map. Take a look below at the network of monitors so far!

PurpleAir Website


Airly has over 4000 sensors installed worldwide. Their online maps integrate trustworthy data about key weather parameters such as temperature, humidity, air pressure and wind along with pollution markers from the locations where the sensors are installed.
